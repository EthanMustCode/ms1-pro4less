Camera

When you start looking into what type of gear you need to shoot your own videos, it becomes all too easy to wind up in a rabbit hole of amazing cameras and equipment. Even when on a budget, it’s tempting to want to spend big bucks to invest in the best gear possible. You don’t need to fork out thousands of pounds on “the best” camera equipment to make a good video. It’s actually never been more affordable to create stunning images on the cheap, and there’s more to a good-looking video than just the camera you use. Here are some affordable alternatives to traditional video equipment: Your Phone – It’s possible to shoot high-quality footage entirely on your phone, especially if you have a phone made within the last 3 years, it’s a perfect fit if your on a very tight budget. DSLR Cameras – if you want a more professional look a DlSR is a perfect place to start. The last couple generations of DSLRs from Canon, Nikon, and more have had amazing video quality, and are still very popular with indie filmmakers. There are plenty of affordable DSLRs like the Nikon 1576 D5600, the Canon EOS 70D, and more under $1,000. Low-Light Cameras – Low-light cameras have special sensors that help them take in more information with less light. This means you can spend less time and money on expensive lighting. The LUMIX GH4 is a great low-light camera on the affordable side. On the expensive side, there’s the absolutely fantastic A7s 3.
